Best Gaming Pheripherals of 2021

January 16 2022

By Cale Hunt

Making a switch to PC to handle your fun is a big step, and it's not complete without some of the best accessories for PC gaming. Whether you've gone with one of the best gaming laptops, best gaming desktop PC, or you've navigated stock shortages to build a custom gaming PC, this collection of hardware will get you set up the right way.

  • Best Monitor: LG UltraGear 27GL850-B monitor

    Your PC gaming experience is only as good as your monitor, and the LG UltraGear 27GL850-B is mighty good. It's a 27-inch screen with 2560x1440 (QHD) resolution, it has a 144Hz refresh rate and 1ms response time, and excellent color and contrast. NVIDIA G-Sync means less tearing, and the stand allows for height, swivel, and tilt.

  • Best Headset: SteelSeries Arctis Pro headset + GameDAC

    This wired gaming headset doesn't come cheap, but it brings unbelievable sound with virtual 7.1 surround, Hi-Res certification, and dedicated DAC with amplifier. The quality is top-notch, the headset is comfortable to wear for hours at a time, and your voice will come through clearly for anyone listening. Check out our Arctis Pro + GameDAC review for more info. $249 at Amazon

  • Best Keyboard: SteelSeries Apex Pro keyboard

    While most gaming keyboards rely on mechanical switches, the SteelSeries Apex Pro goes its own way and uses magnetic actuation that can be tuned on a per-key basis to get exactly the performance and feel you need. Per-key RGB lighting, included wrist rest, built-in control display, and overall high build quality make this keyboard worth every dollar.

  • Best Wired Mouse: Razer Viper 8KHz mouse

    The Razer Viper 8K features mechanical switches for the clickers, 5G optical sensor, and 8K polling rate for exceptional performance. It's comfortable, it's built to the usual Razer standard, and it's ideal for competitive play. If you want a wired gaming mouse, this is the one to get.

  • Best Wireless Mouse: Logitech G502 Lightspeed mouse

    If you want to ditch the wires, Logitech's G502 Lightspeed is the way to go. It was a HERO 16K sensor and 1ms report rate, with support for up to 16,000 DPI. The mouse is comfortable to use for long periods, it's built well, and it's going to keep up with your skill despite its wireless nature.

  • Best Gaming Earbuds: Razer Hammerhead True Wireless Pro earbuds

    Razer's new Hammerhead earbuds are a true alternative to a full headset for gamers. They bring active noise canceling, THX certification, customizable EQ, and low latency despite their wireless nature. Your voice comes through clear thanks to built-in microphones, and the buds come with lots of extra tips so that you can find a comfortable fit.

  • Best Gamepad: New Xbox Wireless Controller

    The new Xbox Wireless Controller works with the Xbox Series X|S and Windows 10, allowing you to game on your PC with just a Bluetooth connection. It's a continuation of the unbeatable design, now with a dedicated share button. If you'd rather forego a mouse and keyboard, this is the best way to go.

  • Best Mousepad: Corsair MM300 Pro

    Not only is the MM300 Pro mousepad from Corsair big enough to handle wide mouse swipes, but it's also spill-proof, so your Mountain Dew doesn't soak in when the can tips over. The bottom is a bit rubbery to prevent it from slipping around, and the top's micro-weave is guaranteed to help your mouse track exceptionally well. $23 at Amazon

After the visual experience comes the aural experience, and we can't recommend enough the SteelSeries Arctis Pro + GameDAC headset package. The audio it delivers thanks to the bundled amp unit and virtual 7.1 surround is unbeatable. These have Hi-Res certification, a noise-canceling microphone, and a ton of comfort, so you don't have to worry about your ears during a long play session. They sit at the top of our best PC gaming headset roundup, and for a good reason, as you can see more about in our SteelSeries Arctis Pro + GameDAC review.
